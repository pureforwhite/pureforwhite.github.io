How to make transition sentence

When you start a new paragraph, the first sentence should clearly express:

  • What this paragraph will discuss
  • How it relates to the previous paragraph

The examples below show some examples of transition sentences between paragraphs and what they express.

  • Further evidence in support of this hypothesis is provided by Smith (2019).
  • However, Patel’s arguments are not the final word on the matter.
  • Having established the relationship between these factors, we are in a position to draw conclusions about the broader process.

You need to know about transition sentence

  • While transitions between paragraphs are generally a single sentence, when you start a new section in a longer text, you may need an entire transition paragraph. Transitioning to a new section involves summarizing the content of the previous section and expressing how the new one will build upon or depart from it.
  • Transition sentence is actually topic sentence. Remember that, Transition sentence is for the whole writing but not for the next sentence. For the next sentence, this is not a transition sentence but a topic sentence.

Classification (將…分類，將…歸類；把…分級)

Break down your main message into smaller categories.

There are problems in applying this approach. First, ability to pay is sometimes difficult to measure. Poverty can be transitory. For example, those in need of social care services may be in transitory poverty. Forcing them to pay could reduce their ability to get out of poverty. Second, some problems are catastrophic in nature. In such cases, the expenditure is great, so that having to pay out of pocket for services would push the non-poor into poverty. Examples of catastrophic problems include a disabling accident, the onset of disease, or illness affecting more than one family member at the same time.

Chronological (按事件發生的年代順序排列的)

After the topic sentence, begin with what happened first and take it from there. The topic sentence can be used to limit the chronological scope of the paragraph.

Uganda’s economic fortunes have varied considerably over the past 40 years according to its political situation. After independence in 1962 and throughout the 1960s, Uganda showed great potential as one of the strongest economies in Sub-Saharan Africa. However, its performance was set back considerably during the subsequent periods of military rule (1971-79) and civil war (1980-85). Then, in 1987 the new National Resistance Movement (NRM) government under the leadership of President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni launched a recovery program to restore financial stability, create conditions for rapid and sustained growth and develop human capital. It also embarked on policy and institutional reform to deregulate the economy, eliminate direct state involvement in all but essential public services, and improve institutional efficiency. These efforts put Uganda on a path of recovery, but progress was slow through the early 1990s.

Evidence and Illustration

Support your topic sentence with examples.

Evidence gathered in the field presents an uneven picture of collaboration and cooperation. For example, Burkina Faso had very little collaboration between the WBI and country team on the provision of training. Mexico had some collaboration. Bangladesh had extensive and fruitful cooperation and collaboration. Differences in the extent of collaboration and cooperation could be traced to different management practices of WBI Regional Coordinators (box 5.3).

Contrast and Comparison

Demonstrate similarities or differences between two or more people or things.

Previously, program beneficiaries were chosen by funders; now the process is improved because beneficiaries are chosen in consultation with the community. In previous years, when the programs were smaller, the funders usually chose program beneficiaries. Local governments would undergo an application process for funding. Now, in selecting the program beneficiaries, criteria are decided jointly with the community leaders in the selected area. For a program to be sustainable, the affected or concerned community should be involved in the project from the design phase onwards. The community should be the decision maker in terms of who can participate in the program. This enhanced ownership can ensure fair distribution of benefits, minimize intra-community conflict over who participates, and thus render programs more sustainable.

Cause and Effect

Make a statement and then show what the effects of that statement would be. The reader can expect to see the results or predictions related to the initial statement.

Because of the recent escalation of energy costs—now the highest in the last ten years—concern has emerged about whether developing countries can sustain high rates of growth. In Central America and East Africa, to take two examples, oil prices have jumped by XX percent in the last year alone, causing price rises in the manufacturing and transportation sectors, which, in turn, have made the countries' exports less competitive. Another effect can be seen in South Asia, where natural gas imports now consume YY percent of the government budget and therefore effectively reduce funding for other sectors. As a result of these soaring energy costs, fewer economists predict that developing nations can look forward to a reasonably promising future.
